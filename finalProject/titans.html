Asteria
Titaness of Falling Stars

Asteria was the daughter of Coeus and Phoebe, associated with falling stars and nocturnal oracles. She transformed into the island of Delos to escape Zeus.

Symbols: Stars, night

Family: Daughter of Coeus and Phoebe; mother of Hecate with Perses.

Astraeus
Titan of Dusk

Astraeus, son of Crius and Eurybia, was the Titan of dusk and father of the winds and stars with Eos.

Symbols: Twilight, stars

Family: Son of Crius and Eurybia; husband of Eos; father of the Anemoi and Astraea.

Atlas
Titan of Endurance

Atlas, son of Iapetus, was condemned to hold up the sky after the Titanomachy, symbolizing strength and burden.

Symbols: Globe, sky

Family: Son of Iapetus and Clymene; father of the Pleiades, Hyades, and Hesperides.

Clymene
Titaness of Fame

Clymene, an Oceanid or Titaness, was the wife of Iapetus and mother of famous Titans like Prometheus and Atlas.

Symbols: Fame, water

Family: Daughter of Oceanus and Tethys; wife of Iapetus; mother of Atlas, Prometheus, Epimetheus, Menoetius.

Coeus
Titan of Intelligence

Coeus represented intellect and the inquisitive mind, often linked to the celestial axis.

Symbols: Stars, mind

Family: Son of Uranus and Gaia; husband of Phoebe; father of Leto and Asteria.

Crius
Titan of Constellations

Crius was associated with the heavens and constellations, fathering notable offspring like Astraeus.

Symbols: Stars, ram

Family: Son of Uranus and Gaia; husband of Eurybia; father of Astraeus, Pallas, Perses.

Cronus
Leader of the Titans

Cronus overthrew Uranus and ruled the Golden Age, later deposed by his son Zeus.

Symbols: Sickle, scythe

Family: Son of Uranus and Gaia; husband of Rhea; father of Zeus, Poseidon, Hades, Hera, Demeter, Hestia.

Dione
Titaness of Oracles

Dione, sometimes considered a Titaness, was linked to the oracle at Dodona and mother of Aphrodite in some myths.

Symbols: Oracle, water

Family: Daughter of Uranus and Gaia or Oceanus and Tethys; mother of Aphrodite with Zeus.

Eos
Titaness of Dawn

Eos, daughter of Hyperion and Thea, heralded the dawn each day, often depicted with rosy fingers.

Symbols: Dawn, wings

Family: Daughter of Hyperion and Thea; mother of the Anemoi with Astraeus.

Epimetheus
Titan of Afterthought

Epimetheus created animals and accepted Pandora as his wife, inadvertently bringing misfortune to mankind.

Symbols: Animals, hindsight

Family: Son of Iapetus and Clymene; husband of Pandora.

Eurybia
Titaness of Sea Mastery

Eurybia, a sea goddess, was the consort of Crius and mother of powerful Titans.

Symbols: Sea, strength

Family: Daughter of Gaia and Pontus; wife of Crius; mother of Astraeus, Pallas, Perses.

Eurynome
Titaness of Pastures

Eurynome, an Oceanid or Titaness, was associated with meadows and ruled the world with Ophion before Cronus.

Symbols: Meadows, water

Family: Daughter of Oceanus and Tethys; mother of the Charites with Zeus.

Hyperion
Titan of Light

Hyperion fathered the celestial deities Helios, Selene, and Eos, embodying light and observation.

Symbols: Sun, light

Family: Son of Uranus and Gaia; husband of Thea; father of Helios, Selene, Eos.

Iapetus
Titan of Mortality

Iapetus was linked to human lifespan, fathering Titans who shaped mankind’s fate.

Symbols: Spear, death

Family: Son of Uranus and Gaia; husband of Clymene; father of Atlas, Prometheus, Epimetheus, Menoetius.

Lelantos
Titan of Air

Lelantos, a lesser-known Titan, was associated with air and stealth, father of Aura.

Symbols: Wind, invisibility

Family: Son of Coeus and Phoebe; father of Aura.

Leto
Titaness of Motherhood

Leto, daughter of Coeus and Phoebe, bore Apollo and Artemis despite Hera’s wrath.

Symbols: Veil, motherhood

Family: Daughter of Coeus and Phoebe; mother of Apollo and Artemis with Zeus.

Menoetius
Titan of Rage

Menoetius, son of Iapetus, was struck down by Zeus for his hubris during the Titanomachy.

Symbols: Lightning, anger

Family: Son of Iapetus and Clymene.

Metis
Titaness of Wisdom

Metis, an Oceanid or Titaness, was Zeus’s first wife, swallowed by him to prevent a prophecy.

Symbols: Owl, cunning

Family: Daughter of Oceanus and Tethys; mother of Athena with Zeus.

Mnemosyne
Titaness of Memory

Mnemosyne mothered the Muses with Zeus, embodying memory and inspiration.

Symbols: Scrolls, water

Family: Daughter of Uranus and Gaia; mother of the Muses with Zeus.

Oceanus
Titan of the Ocean

Oceanus ruled the great river encircling the world, staying neutral in the Titanomachy.

Symbols: Water, serpent

Family: Son of Uranus and Gaia; husband of Tethys; father of the Oceanids and river gods.

Ophion
Titan of Serpents

Ophion, an early ruler with Eurynome, was overthrown by Cronus and Rhea in some myths.

Symbols: Serpent, chaos

Family: Consort of Eurynome.

Pallas
Titan of Warfare

Pallas, son of Crius, was a Titan of warlike prowess, killed by Athena in some tales.

Symbols: Spear, goat

Family: Son of Crius and Eurybia; husband of Styx; father of Nike, Kratos, Zelos, Bia.

Perses
Titan of Destruction

Perses embodied destruction and was the father of Hecate with Asteria.

Symbols: Fire, ruin

Family: Son of Crius and Eurybia; husband of Asteria; father of Hecate.

Phoebe
Titaness of Prophecy

Phoebe held the Oracle of Delphi before Apollo, symbolizing prophetic wisdom.

Symbols: Moon, oracle

Family: Daughter of Uranus and Gaia; wife of Coeus; mother of Leto, Asteria.

Prometheus
Titan of Forethought

Prometheus stole fire for humanity, earning eternal punishment from Zeus.

Symbols: Fire, torch

Family: Son of Iapetus and Clymene.

Rhea
Titaness of Fertility

Rhea hid Zeus from Cronus, aiding the Olympians’ rise to power.

Symbols: Crown, lion

Family: Daughter of Uranus and Gaia; wife of Cronus; mother of Zeus, Poseidon, Hades, Hera, Demeter, Hestia.

Selene
Titaness of the Moon

Selene, daughter of Hyperion and Thea, drove the moon across the night sky.

Symbols: Moon, crescent

Family: Daughter of Hyperion and Thea; lover of Endymion.

Styx
Titaness of Oaths

Styx, an Oceanid or Titaness, was the river of oaths, siding with Zeus in the Titanomachy.

Symbols: River, oath

Family: Daughter of Oceanus and Tethys; wife of Pallas; mother of Nike, Kratos, Zelos, Bia.

Tethys
Titaness of Fresh Water

Tethys nurtured the rivers and streams as the wife of Oceanus.

Symbols: Water, wings

Family: Daughter of Uranus and Gaia; wife of Oceanus; mother of the Oceanids and river gods.

Thea (Theia)
Titaness of Sight

Thea (also Theia) was associated with sight and brilliance, mother of the sun, moon, and dawn.

Symbols: Light, eyes

Family: Daughter of Uranus and Gaia; wife of Hyperion; mother of Helios, Selene, Eos.

Themis
Titaness of Divine Law

Themis personified justice and order, mothering the Fates and Seasons with Zeus.

Symbols: Scales, sword

Family: Daughter of Uranus and Gaia; wife of Zeus; mother of the Horae and Moirai.
