Zeus
King of the Gods, God of Sky, Thunder, Lightning, Law, Order, Justice

Zeus, the supreme ruler of Olympus, led his siblings to overthrow the Titans. He wields the thunderbolt and governs divine law, but his numerous affairs often sparked conflicts.

Symbols: Thunderbolt, eagle, oak tree, bull, scales, scepter.

Roman Counterpart: Jupiter

Family: Son of Cronus and Rhea; husband to Hera; father of many gods and heroes.

Athena
Goddess of Wisdom, Strategy, Warfare, Crafts

Born from Zeus's forehead, Athena is the patron of Athens, known for her strategic prowess and patronage of crafts like weaving.

Symbols: Owl, olive tree, snake, Aegis, armor, spear.

Roman Counterpart: Minerva

Family: Daughter of Zeus and Metis.

Cronus
Titan of Time, Harvest

Cronus overthrew his father Uranus and ruled during the Golden Age. He was later deposed by his son Zeus.

Symbols: Sickle, grain, scythe.

Roman Counterpart: Saturn

Family: Son of Uranus and Gaia; husband of Rhea; father of Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Hades, Hestia, Demeter.

Rhea
Titaness of Fertility, Motherhood, Generation

Rhea, the mother of the Olympian gods, saved Zeus from Cronus by hiding him and tricking her husband.

Symbols: Lion, drum, crown, cornucopia.

Roman Counterpart: Ops

Family: Daughter of Uranus and Gaia; wife of Cronus; mother of Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Hades, Hestia, Demeter.

Gaia
Primordial Goddess of the Earth

Gaia, the ancestral mother of all life, gave birth to the Titans and other primordial beings, shaping the cosmos.

Symbols: Earth, fruit, grain, mountains.

Roman Counterpart: Terra

Family: Mother of Uranus, Pontus, Titans, and others; wife of Uranus.

Uranus
Primordial God of the Sky

Uranus, the sky god, was the first ruler of the cosmos until overthrown by his son Cronus at Gaia's urging.

Symbols: Sky, stars, heavens.

Roman Counterpart: Caelus

Family: Son and husband of Gaia; father of Titans, Cyclopes, Hecatoncheires.

Hades
God of the Underworld, the Dead, Riches

Hades rules the underworld, overseeing the dead. Known for abducting Persephone, he is just but stern.

Symbols: Cerberus, Helm of Darkness, cypress, narcissus, key, bident.

Roman Counterpart: Pluto

Family: Son of Cronus and Rhea; husband of Persephone.

Thanatos
God of Death

Thanatos personifies peaceful death, guiding souls to the underworld alongside his twin Hypnos.

Symbols: Butterfly, poppy, sword, inverted torch.

Roman Counterpart: Mors

Family: Son of Nyx and Erebus; twin of Hypnos.

Pan
God of the Wild, Shepherds, Flocks, Rustic Music

Pan, with his goat-like features, roams the wilds, playing his pan pipes and protecting shepherds.

Symbols: Pan pipes, goat, fir tree.

Roman Counterpart: Faunus

Family: Son of Hermes and a nymph.

Nereus
Old Man of the Sea

Nereus, a wise sea god, is known for his truthfulness and shapeshifting, fathering the Nereids.

Symbols: Fish, trident, seaweed.

Roman Counterpart: Nereus

Family: Son of Pontus and Gaia; father of the Nereids.
