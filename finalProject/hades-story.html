Hades and the Underworld

In the shadowed depths of the Underworld, Hades reigned, his throne carved from obsidian. Lord of the dead, he watched over souls with a gaze as cold as the rivers Styx and Acheron. At his side, Cerberus, the three-headed hound, growled, ensuring no shade escaped the eternal dusk.

Unlike his brothers, Hades shunned the light of Olympus, finding purpose in his grim domain. He judged the dead with unyielding fairness, sending the virtuous to Elysium and the wicked to Tartarus' torments. His Helm of Darkness cloaked him in shadow, a symbol of his unseen power.

Mortals feared him, yet Hades was no cruel tyrant. He upheld the balance of life and death, his rule a necessity for the cosmos. Even gods tread carefully in his realm, for his word was law where light dared not linger.

Hades' kingdom endured, a silent testament to his sovereignty. Beneath the earth, he sat eternal, the unseen king whose justice bound the dead and steadied the world above.

Key Figures: Hades, Cerberus, Zeus, Charon

Symbols: Helm of Darkness, Pomegranate, Cerberus
